Advisory for debates, arguments, and disagreements
  • Establish points of mutual agreement.
  • Get an idea where the other person is coming from - do not jump to conclusions rashly.
  • Make sure others are aware of the context behind any ideological framework you are using - they might not be familiar. If such is the case, consider providing a more generalized argument, or conveying the presuppositions in your argument.
  • Leave a way out, and give room for people to admit their mistakes. Don't rub things in, trample on people's dignity, and don't force someone to partake in an argument they're no longer interested in. Also, give people time to collect their thoughts, or continue later, and don't rush them. Discussing things here should be an enjoyable activity. If someone is no longer enjoying themselves, then give them some space.
  • Consider how someone's argument might be correct before considering how it may be incorrect. Provide both sides of your analysis in any counter argument.
  • Don't double down when facing difficult arguments - loosen up. Relax and think things through. If one must, consider the possibility of being wrong. People as a whole are good at finding the right answer to things. One's opponent being right is to be expected, for the most part. It is not a failure of oneself, nor is it a sign of inferiority. It is an opportunity for learning and intellectual growth.
  • Respect a good argument. Show respect if someone comes out on top in a debate. The important thing is that they were willing to debate in the first place. If poor sportsmanship is shown, they might not be willing to do so in the future.
-be honest and sincere, even to yourself. (This is more of a non binding important advice hehe)
  • Have fun. This is mandatory and will be enforced by the fun police. The punishment for violating this guideline is state-enforced boredom.
